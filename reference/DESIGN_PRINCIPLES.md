# DESIGN PRINCIPLES

## Core Layout Philosophy
- Avoid default template-like split layouts unless strategically justified.
- Do not push important hero content to opposite outer edges by default.
- Prefer stronger visual grouping and central focal clustering for premium layouts.
- Design should feel composed, intentional, and art-directed — not auto-generated.

## Spacing & Rhythm
- Use generous spacing, but ensure related content maintains visual relationship.
- Avoid arbitrary dead space that disconnects content blocks.
- Prioritize consistent vertical rhythm across sections.
- Let sections breathe, but avoid emptiness without compositional purpose.

## Composition
- Favor editorial asymmetry over rigid repetitive symmetry where appropriate.
- Break mechanical grids when it improves hierarchy or visual interest.
- Avoid overly equal-width columns unless strategically necessary.
- Layout should feel curated, not mathematically generic.

## Hierarchy
- Make section priorities immediately clear through scale and spacing.
- Avoid flattening all cards/sections into visually equal importance.
- Establish clear focal points in every major section.

## Luxury / Premium Design Direction
- Premium design should prioritize restraint over excess.
- Use fewer, stronger elements rather than many competing elements.
- Favor sophistication and confidence over flashy gimmicks.
- Premium layouts should feel deliberate and spacious.

## Conversion Page Principles
- Balance luxury branding with conversion clarity.
- Never sacrifice usability for aesthetics.
- Calls to action should feel integrated, not bolted on.
- Trust-building elements should feel curated and credible, not generic.

## Avoid
- Generic SaaS-style card grids unless justified.
- Default left-text/right-image templates without refinement.
- Overly mechanical section repetition.
- Excessive symmetry that makes layouts feel templated.
- Weak focal hierarchy.
